1. Noun

What is noun ?
Noun is a Naming word person,things,place.
Four types of noun
  1. Proper noun - specific names
    2. Ex: Kumar , Gokul , neem tree etc.
  2. common noun - name in general
    3. Ex: Man , Women , bird , tree etc.
  3. collective noun - names of group
    4. Ex: team , people , audience etc.
  4. Meterial noun - names of meterial
    5. Ex: gold , silver , iron etc.
Abstract noun
  1. countable noun
    2. Ex: apple , bus , mobile etc.
  2. uncountable noun
    3. Ex: hair , rice , star etc.

7. Conjunction

What is conjunction ?
A word that is used to join two words, phrases or sentences is called a conjunction.
  1. Coordinating conjunctions :
    2.  A conjunction that joins together two sentences or clauses of equal rank. It is used to make compound sentence.
    Common Coordinating conjunctions :
    and , but , so , or , yet , still , whereas and while.
  2. Subordinating Conjunctions :
    3.  A conjunction that connects a main clause and a subordinate clause together. It is used to make complex sentences.
    Common Subordinating Conjunctions :
    though , since , as , if , unless , that , as soon as , when , till and than.
  3. Correlative conjunctions :
    4.  Conjunctions which are used in pairs.
    Common Correlative conjunctions :
    either... or , neither...nor , not only... but also , not...but , whether...or and so...that

8. Interjection

What is interjection ?
Interjection is a word that is used to express sudden feelings and emotions of joy, sorrow, surprise, shock, disappointment and contempt. An exclamation mark (!) usually follows such expressions.
Look at the following sentences :
    expression of joy
  • Hurray! Our college team won the final!
    • expression of sorrow
  • Alas! How many people are dying of Corona virus!
    • expression of surprise at sudden finding
  • Oh! This is your plan!
    • expression of surprise
  • Aah! What a lovely place!
    • expression of contempt at somebody's noise
  • Shh! My baby is sleeping!
    • expression of shock
  • Oh my God! I forgot my hall ticket!
    • expression of disapproval
  • Shit! What nonsense you are doing!
    • expression of anger
  • Damn it! I'll kill you!

Functional units

  1. Subject
  2. Verb
  3. Object
  4. Complement
  5. Adjunct

  1. subject ( s ) :

    2. Ask the question Who?/ Which? to the verb the answer is subject ( s ).
    Ex : Ragu is crying.
    ( Who is crying? )
    The dog is barking.
    ( Which is barking )

  2. verb ( V ) :

    3. main verb or auxiliary verb
    Ex : My friends submitted the project.

  3. object ( O ) :

    4. Ask the question- What?/ Whom? to the verb The answer is Object ( o ).
    Ex : We booked a movie ticket.
    (we booked - What?)
    She contacted the manager at once.
    (She contacted - whom?)
    Direct object (DO) :
    Ask the question What? The answer is direct object.
    Ex : My father gifted- what? The answer 'a watch' is a direct object here.
    Indirect object (IO) :
    ask the question whom? the answer is indirect object (IO).
    Ex : My father gifted a watch - to whom? The answer 'to me' is an indirect object here.

  4. complement (C) :

    5. That which completes the meaning of a sentence is complement.
    Ex : She is our teacher.
    (without 'our teacher the sentence is incomplete)
    What somebody has is complement.
    Ex : He has two calculators.
    (Ask What he has and the answer is complement)
    What something has is complement.
    Ex : A dog has four legs.
    (Ask what the dog has and the answer is complement)
    How something is or appears is complement.
    Ex : Meena is beautiful. / Meenia looks beautiful.
    (Ask how Meena is or how Meena looks, and the answer is complement.)
    How something is or something appears is complement.
    Ex : It is true / It sounds true.
    (Ask how something is or how something appears to be, and the answer is complement)

  5. Adjunct ( A ) :

    6. ask the question
    when?
    Ex : We conducted the meeting yesterday. ( When? Yesterday = adjunct)
    where?
    Ex : I met him in Chennai. ( Where? in Chennai = adjunct)
    why?
    Ex : I met him to get back my mobile. ( Why? to get back my mobile = adjunct)
    how?
    Ex : He explained the thesis clearly. ( How did he explain? clearly= adjunct)

Model Verb

Model Verb Where to use ? Example
Shall a) To express future tense (first person) I shall do it for you.
b) To express strong assertion (second and third person) They shall dismiss him from the job.
c) To express instruction Every applicant shall enclose a self- addressed envelope.
d) Used in question to make offer or suggestion Shall I make a call? Shall we go now?
Will a) To express future tense ( II and III person) She will talk to you in the evening.
b) To express strong assertion ( I person) I'm sure, I will win.
c) To express willingness I'll take you to the station. I'll give you a gift.
d) To invite/request someone politely to do something Will you join us for a cup of coffee?
e) To pass an order Will you go upstairs now?
Should a) Used to show what is right and appropriate You should not drink and drive.
b) To give or ask for advice You should consult a doctor first. Whom should I invite first?
c) To express refusal or anger Why should I help him? What should I do now?
d) To give opinion that you are not certain about Is this enough for everyone? I should think so. Will it work out? I shouldn't think so.
would a) Used as the past form of will in reported speech and if - clause He said he would come late. if you asked for help, she would help you.
b) To refer to a possible happening They would like to avoid him. It would be of no use. They would have come now.
c) used in polite offer / invitation Would you like coffee? Would you like sweets?
d) To make polite requests Would you mind leaving me alone for a while? Would you mind switching off your mobile now?
May a) To express possibility What he says may be true. We may receive the order this week.
b) Used to ask for / give permission May I come in madam? You may go now.
c) To express a wish May God bless you. May his soul rest in peace.
d) To ask for information May I know your good name, please?
Might a) Used as the past form of may in reported speech We thought that he might leave early.
b) To point out other's fault politely, especially what they haven't done You might have informed me earlier.
c) To make suggestions You might try pain killers, why don't you?
d) To express strong possibility He might reject your proposal.
Can a) To express ability, capacity I can speak five languages. The room can accommodate only 30 students.
b) To indicate that something is permitted You can use your mobile here at any time. You can sit anywhere you want.
c) To make a request to friends or juniors Can you wait for me for a while? Can you remind me of this tomorrow?
d) To make a suggestion or offer help We can try somewhere else. Can I help you?
e) To ask for permission (to friends, juniors) Can I use your mobile?
f) To express possibility Smoking can cause cancer. Nobody can save you, if you do it again.
Must a) To express necessity stronger than 'should' You must send your application before 5 p.m We must know the right password to access this.
b) To express opinions logically formed You must be tired now after working for two shifts.He must be in. Didn't you hear his voice?
c) Before sharing realizations I must admit, he is not a good choice for you. I must say, his performance is more than what expected.
ought a) To express duty You ought to be punctual on your duty.
b) To express what is legally or morally right We ought to obey the traffic rules. You ought to help the poor.
c) To express what is desirable or ideal You ought to have basic computer knowledge to work here. There ought to be a solar panel for each house.

simple present

used for facts, generalizations, and truths that are not affected by the passage of time.

[V/Vs]

Example :

He eat biriyani.

I travel in bus.

Kathir speak English well.

Simple past

used for events completed in the past.

[V (past form)]

Example :

He aet chicken in yesterday.

He traveled in Chennai.

He went college in Monday.

simple future

used for events to be completed in the future.

[Will + V]

Example :

I will go to college.

I will travel in bus.

I will reach qukly in the college.

Present continuous

used to describe currently ongoing (usually temporary) actions.

[am/is/are + V ing form]

Example :

I am driving in cycle.

He is going to college.

They are working on the road.

Past continuous

used to describe ongoing past events, often in relation to the occurrence of another event.

[was/were + V ing form]

Example :

He was eating in breakfast.

They were watching Movie.

Kathir was working our land in yesterday.

Future continuous

used to describe future events that are expected to continue over a period of time.

[will be + V ing form]

Example :

Kathir will be studying BE course after diploma.

He will be writing record note in tomorrow.

The Board exam will be starting in April 25.

Present perfect

used to describe events that began in the past and are expected to continue, or to emphasize the relevance of past events to the present moment.

[Have/Has + V3 (Past participle form)]

Example :

I have gotten a mobile.

He has called him.

I have purchased fruits.

Past perfect

used to describe events that happened prior to other events in the past.

[had + V3 (Past participle form)]

Example :

I had bought bike in yesterday.

She had gone to supermarket.

He had ate chicken rice in yesterday evening.

Future perfect

used to describe events that will be completed between now and a specific point in the future.

[Will have + V3 (past participle form)]

Example :

He will have driven car.

she will have drunk milk.

He will have given a pen.

Present perfect continuous

used to describe events that started in the past and continue into the present or were recently completed, emphasizing their relevance to the present moment.

[have/has been + V ing form]

Example :

I have been reading book.

He has been working police station.

She has been cooking dinner.

Past perfect continuous

used to describe events that began, continued, and ended in the past, emphasizing their relevance to a past moment.

[had been + V ing form]

Example :

He had been asking questions.

She had been driving mango juice.

I hadn't been eating chicken rice.

Future perfect continuous

used to describe events that will continue up until a point in the future, emphasizing their expected duration.

[Will have been + V ing form]

Example :

He will have been playing games in tomorrow.

She will have been using mobile.

He will have been acting movie.
